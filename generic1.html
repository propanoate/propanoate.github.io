Essence - No Exit

Written by A.G. and G.W.

Essence is an important idea in Sartre's No Exit, as Sartre's existentialist philosophy deals heavily on what it means to be. (Note that essence contains "esse", the Latin verb for "to be".) No Exit is deeply connected to Sartre's existentialist beliefs , and this can first and foremost be seen through Sartre's famous phrase, "existence precedes essence." As an existentialist, Sartre believed that your actions define you. One's actions -- the choices that one commits to in life -- defines you. As a consequence of this , Sartre believes that each individual owes a high level of responsibility to each's actions. And as a result, you are haunted by the effects of your actions.

The tragedy of the situation, and why each of the characters in Sartre's No Exit is condemned to suffer in hell forever, is that each person is trying to change or acquire an aspect of another person, when the essence of everyone in the room has already been decided. Each character can’t change the essence of others and as a result each is debilitated and unable to escape or alter their lives in hell.
